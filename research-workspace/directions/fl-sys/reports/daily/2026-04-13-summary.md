# 每日想法总结: 2026-04-13

## 今日核心判断
异构 FL 正在通过 *自适应模型容量*（剪枝/深度）与 *分层/标签感知聚合* 两条路线推进——但系统级成本核算、公平性以及协议兼容性仍是主要缺口。

## 关键结论
- 自适应容量 FL 正在收敛到一种通用范式：保留共享“超网络（supernet）”，让客户端训练相互兼容的子模型（剪枝或更浅），再进行参与感知的聚合以避免更新不匹配。
- 这些论文中与 FL 系统最相关的关键杠杆点包括：(i) 如何聚合部分重叠的参数集合，(ii) 在截止时间/拖尾客户端（stragglers）约束下如何为每个客户端调度容量，(iii) 如何让这些决策在非 IID + 部分参与下依然稳健。
- 分层 FL + 知识聚合（标签驱动、teacher–student）被视为一条可扩展路径，同时在 *群组/边缘* 层面缓解非 IID——提示其可作为客户端级容量自适应之外的一条互补轴。
- 跨所有工作，可能缺失的“关键胜利”是端到端系统证据：线上字节数（含 mask/元数据）、真实设备上的端到端时钟时间/能耗，以及与安全聚合（secure aggregation）和 churn 的兼容性。
- 一个反复出现的风险：深层/未剪枝参数只由高资源客户端更新 → 容量偏置、公平性问题，并可能对少数（往往非 IID）子集过拟合，除非显式校正。

## 好问题，弱方案
- FedP3 (2404.09816)：问题很重要（设备异构 + 参与性），但基于剪枝的方案在实践中往往较弱：若稀疏性是非结构化的，或未计入 mask 协调开销；此外也不清楚服务器端状态如何随每客户端 mask 扩展。
- Distributed Pruning (2212.01977)：问题很重要（面向 FL 的极小网络），但“分布式 mask 发现”在 churn/部分参与下往往变得脆弱；若 mask/索引未被高效编码，还可能抹平通信收益；在全局 mask 下公平性/尾部准确率可能下降。
- Adaptive Dual Side Depth (2309.13536)：问题刻画很强（数据+设备异构相互纠缠）；风险在于深度截断会导致更深模块参与稀疏，并可能使收敛不稳定，除非聚合权重与更新差异校正被严格设计并在动态条件下评估。
- Label-Driven Knowledge Aggregator in HFL (2209.14520)：问题很强（非 IID 鲁棒性 + 可扩展性），但标签驱动方案可能带来隐私泄露风险，并在标签空间不一致或随时间演化时变得脆弱；也常不清楚在没有用于蒸馏的公共/代理数据时收益是否仍能保持。

## 值得继续想
- 2309.13536v4 (Adaptive Dual Side Depth)：与 FL-sys 高度匹配——若其确实做到了 (a) 深度分配、(b) 差异感知/加权聚合、(c) 切换点策略的协同设计则很有前景。后续想法：在延迟/带宽约束 + 深层参与公平性约束下，做每轮的 deadline-aware 优化来选择（客户端集合、深度）。
- 2209.14520v2 (Label-Driven Knowledge Aggregator, HFL)：与可扩展部署高度匹配——值得关注其 *知识载荷（knowledge payload）设计* 以及是否报告分层级（tier-by-tier）的通信/计算成本。后续想法：隐私保护的标签驱动聚合（DP/secure-agg 直方图）+ 对被操纵的标签统计的鲁棒性。
- 2404.09816v1 (FedP3)：值得快速浏览其具体的 mask/聚合协议以及剪枝是否为结构化；后续想法：用基于（预算、设备遥测）的低维 mask 生成器替代显式的每客户端 mask，以降低服务器状态并支持动态预算。
- 2212.01977v2 (Distributed Pruning)：值得检查其是否有原则性的“pool”机制（pool_size 敏感性暗示这一点），可将其改造为生产级 FL 中对 straggler/churn 更鲁棒的剪枝 oracle 服务。

## 重复出现的主题
- 将容量自适应作为主要系统旋钮（深度/宽度/剪枝），以在不维护多套架构的情况下让低端客户端持续参与。
- Masked/部分聚合作为核心技术瓶颈：当客户端只触及不同参数/模块子集时，如何组合更新。
- 耦合异构性（设备↔数据）与由此产生的选择偏置：方法必须处理“谁能训练更多”与“其持有何种数据”之间的相关性。
- 对协议兼容性的需求：安全聚合通常假设固定形状向量；可变深度/剪枝更新需要 padding/masking 或按层进行 secure-agg 分组。
- 评估缺口：参数量节省常被反复用作通信/时延的代理指标，但真实收益取决于结构化稀疏、编码开销与设备端 kernel 支持。

## English Version

# Daily Idea Summary: 2026-04-13

## Headline
Heterogeneous FL is being attacked via *adaptive model capacity* (pruning/depth) and *hierarchical/label-aware aggregation*—but systems-grade accounting, fairness, and protocol compatibility remain the main gaps.

## Top Takeaways
- Adaptive-capacity FL is converging on a common pattern: keep a shared “supernet,” let clients train compatible submodels (pruned or shallower), then do participation-aware aggregation to avoid update mismatch.
- The most FL-sys-relevant leverage points across papers are (i) how to aggregate partially-overlapping parameter sets, (ii) how to schedule per-client capacity under deadlines/stragglers, and (iii) how to make these choices robust to non-IID + partial participation.
- Hierarchical FL + knowledge aggregation (label-driven, teacher–student) is framed as a scalability path that also tackles non-IID at the *group/edge* level—suggesting a complementary axis to client-level capacity adaptation.
- Across all works, the likely “missing win” is end-to-end systems evidence: bytes-on-wire (incl. mask/metadata), wall-clock/energy on real devices, and compatibility with secure aggregation and churn.
- A recurring risk: deep layers / unpruned parameters get updated by only high-resource clients → capacity bias, fairness issues, and overfitting to a small (often non-IID) subset unless explicitly corrected.

## Good Problem, Weak Solution
- FedP3 (2404.09816): Great problem (device heterogeneity + participation) but pruning-based solutions are often weak in practice if sparsity is unstructured or mask coordination overhead isn’t accounted for; also unclear how server state scales with per-client masks.
- Distributed Pruning (2212.01977): Great problem (tiny networks for FL) but “distributed mask discovery” often becomes brittle under churn/partial participation and can erase communication gains if masks/indices aren’t encoded efficiently; fairness/tail accuracy can degrade under global masks.
- Adaptive Dual Side Depth (2309.13536): Strong problem framing (intertwined data+device heterogeneity); risk is that depth truncation creates sparse participation for deeper blocks and can destabilize convergence unless aggregation weights and update-discrepancy corrections are rigorously designed + evaluated under dynamic conditions.
- Label-Driven Knowledge Aggregator in HFL (2209.14520): Strong problem (non-IID robustness + scalability), but label-driven schemes can be privacy-leaky and fragile when label spaces are inconsistent or evolve; often unclear whether gains persist without public/proxy data for distillation.

## Worth Further Thought
- 2309.13536v4 (Adaptive Dual Side Depth): High-match for FL-sys—promising if it truly co-designs (a) depth assignment, (b) discrepancy-aware/weighted aggregation, and (c) a switching-point policy. Follow-up idea: deadline-aware per-round optimization choosing (client set, depth) under latency/bandwidth constraints + fairness constraints for deep-layer participation.
- 2209.14520v2 (Label-Driven Knowledge Aggregator, HFL): High-match for scalable deployments—worth reading for its *knowledge payload design* and whether it reports tier-by-tier comm/compute. Follow-up idea: privacy-preserving label-driven aggregation (DP/secure-agg histograms) + robustness to manipulated label stats.
- 2404.09816v1 (FedP3): Worth scanning for concrete mask/aggregation protocol and whether pruning is structured; follow-up idea: replace explicit per-client masks with a low-dim mask generator conditioned on (budget, device telemetry) to reduce server state and support dynamic budgets.
- 2212.01977v2 (Distributed Pruning): Worth checking if it has a principled “pool” mechanism (pool_size sensitivity hints this) that could be repurposed into a straggler/churn-robust pruning-oracle service in production FL.

## Recurring Themes
- Capacity adaptation as the primary systems knob (depth/width/pruning) to keep low-end clients participating without maintaining many separate architectures.
- Masked/partial aggregation as the core technical bottleneck: how to combine updates when clients touch different subsets of parameters/blocks.
- Coupled heterogeneity (device↔data) and the resulting selection bias: methods must handle the fact that who can train more is correlated with what data they hold.
- Need for protocol compatibility: secure aggregation assumes fixed-shaped vectors; variable-depth/pruned updates require padding/masking or per-layer secure-agg groups.
- Evaluation gap: parameter-count savings are repeatedly used as a proxy for comm/latency, but real wins depend on structured sparsity, encoding overhead, and device kernels.
