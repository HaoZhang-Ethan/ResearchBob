# Daily Idea Summary: 2026-04-10

## Headline
Early GPU-FHE inference remains a strong problem area: HCNN is a useful baseline, but mainly shows feasibility rather than a durable solution.

## Top Takeaways
- HCNN is highly aligned with the current gpu-fhe intake: it directly targets encrypted CNN inference with GPU acceleration.
- The paper’s main value is as an early systems baseline showing the joint role of HE-friendly model design, ciphertext packing, and GPU-offloaded arithmetic.
- It appears to demonstrate that GPU acceleration can help core FHE kernels, but end-to-end practicality is still limited, especially on CIFAR-10.
- For current idea discovery, the unresolved gap is not just faster kernels, but co-design across scheme choice, packing/layout, rotations/key switching, and accelerator scheduling.

## Good Problem, Weak Solution
- Good problem: privacy-preserving CNN inference over encrypted data with hardware acceleration. Weakness: the achieved end-to-end latency is still very high for anything beyond small tasks.
- Good problem: exploit GPU parallelism for FHE workloads. Weakness: likely accelerates arithmetic kernels without fully solving layout, automorphism/rotation, and memory-movement bottlenecks.
- Good problem: large-batch encrypted inference via SIMD packing. Weakness: batching claims depend on slot utilization and may not translate to realistic mixed workloads or modern models.
- Good problem: practical DLaaS privacy. Weakness: solution relies on HE-constrained network design and relatively old security/performance assumptions (>80-bit, likely leveled BFV-style setup).

## Worth Further Thought
- Revisit this design with a modern GPU-first HE stack and isolate where GPU speedups actually come from: NTT, key switching, base conversion, rotations, or data movement.
- Directly compare BFV-style exact inference vs CKKS-style approximate inference on GPU under modern security targets and identical model topologies.
- Treat ciphertext packing/layout as a compiler/search problem jointly optimized for GPU execution, slot utilization, and rotation count.
- Explore multi-GPU decomposition across CRT primes, ciphertext groups, or pipeline stages for encrypted inference workloads.
- Build a standardized gpu-fhe benchmark suite with profiler traces and fixed security levels so future systems can be compared apples-to-apples.

## Recurring Themes
- GPU-FHE performance is a co-design problem, not just a faster-kernel problem.
- Packing/SIMD is central, but its benefits are tightly coupled to layout efficiency and rotation overhead.
- End-to-end encrypted ML performance is limited by non-multiply bottlenecks such as key switching, automorphisms, and memory traffic.
- Older HE inference systems often show feasibility on small benchmarks but struggle to generalize to larger models or modern deployment expectations.
- A recurring intake-relevant theme is the gap between cryptographic primitive acceleration and real application-level wins.
